CH 16 · CONTEXT & BACKGROUND

From Price-Only to Volume-Weighted

Author's Lens:
Buff Dormeier didn't just study these indicators—he invented them. He formulated "Buff Averages" (later named Volume-Weighted Moving Averages, or VWMA) in the late 1990s and published them in 2001. He subsequently created the VW-MACD and TTI while managing portfolios at T.P. Donovan Investments.

Historical Paradigm Shift:
Since Charles Dow, technical analysis largely separated price trends and volume confirmation. Analysts visually inspected volume bars below price charts. The creation of VWMA mathematically married the two, shifting the paradigm from "Price + Volume (visual)" to "Price * Volume (computed)."

Evolution of Ideas (Then vs Now):
In 1999, VWMA was revolutionary. By 2026, VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price) is an institutional standard for algorithmic execution. However, Dormeier's specific TTI and adaptive smoothing concepts remain underutilized alpha generators.

CH 16 · MENTAL MODELS

Trend Thrust Indicator (TTI)

Trend Thrust Indicator (TTI)

Enhancing MACD through volume-weighting, volume multipliers, and an adaptive signal line.
How It Works:
  • The Buff Spread: Replaces MACD's exponential averages with VWMAs, then applies a squared volume multiplier.
  • The Adaptive Signal Line: Lengthens (smooths) when volume increases, delaying false exits. Shortens (tightens) when volume decreases, generating faster counter-trend exits. Ch 16, p. 173
When to Apply:
  • High-volatility, high-volume stocks where trend confirmation is paramount.
⚠️ Limitations & Caveats:
  • Computationally intense to build from scratch.
  • Low-volume large caps may not yield a massive edge over traditional MACD.

CH 16 · EVIDENCE EVALUATION

The 60-Stock Study

Volume Weighting Improves Trading System Results (The 60-Stock Study) ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ 4/5
📊 Empirical Data

Methodology: Dormeier backtested SMA vs VWMA, and MACD vs TTI over 3,000 trading days (2,000 for mid/small caps). 60 stocks across 12 subgroups (capitalization + volume/beta combinations). Ch 16, p. 175

Strengths: Highly structured testing isolating specific variables (volume, beta, cap). Comprehensive sample size yielding statistically significant profitability delta ($261,403 improvement for TTI over MACD).

Weaknesses: Backtest is historical; does not account for slippage or exact trading fees.

2026 Context: Validated daily. Institutional reliance on VWAP for algorithmic entry/exit corroborates the supremacy of volume-weighting over price-only averages.

CH 16 · COUNTERARGUMENTS

The Price-Only & Dark Pool Critiques

Dormeier's View

Volume data contains distinct, leading information that price averages ignore. Injecting volume directly into the moving average formula forces the indicator to reflect actual market conviction, reducing risk and increasing return. Ch 16, p. 181

The 2026 Algorithmic Critique

Dark Pools & Internalization: Critics argue that in modern markets, up to 40-50% of volume is routed off-exchange (dark pools). Therefore, relying on "lit" volume for VWMA calculations may skew indicators toward retail noise rather than institutional conviction.

Synthesis: The solution isn't to abandon VWMA, but to ensure your data feed aggregates consolidated tape volume, and to supplement analysis with Dark Index (DIX) metrics.

CH 16 · READING LAYERS

Depths of Volume Weighting

Layer 1: Surface
Replacing a Simple Moving Average (SMA) with a Volume-Weighted Moving Average (VWMA) improves trading results.

Layer 2: Structural
The classic paradox—fast indicators generate false signals—is fundamentally solved. Volume provides a filtering mechanism that validates speed.

Layer 3: Contextual
Dormeier's creation of VWMA in the late 90s bridged the gap between visual volume analysis (Dow, Wyckoff) and modern computed oscillators (Appel, Wilder).

Layer 4: Generative
If volume weighting works, what about Volatility Weighting? What if the adaptive signal line in TTI incorporated Average True Range (ATR) as an additional multiplier? The architecture is highly extensible.

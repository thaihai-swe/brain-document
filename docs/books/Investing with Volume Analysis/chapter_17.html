CH 17 · CONTEXT & BACKGROUND

The Evolution of Volume Metrics

The Evolution of Volume Metrics

Author's Lens: Buff Dormeier developed the VPCI (Volume Price Confirmation Indicator) as a proprietary method to identify securities experiencing positive price-volume characteristics over long trends. His approach relies heavily on systematic backtesting to validate concepts.

Historical Context: Discovered around 2002, the VPCI successfully signaled major intermediate-term broad market bottoms (like the 2003 V-bottom and the massive March 2009 bottom). It emerged in an era where algorithmic and quantitative validations of technical indicators became essential.

Intellectual Tradition: The VPCI builds upon the foundation of Joe Granville's On-Balance Volume (OBV). While OBV aggregates raw volume flows historically, VPCI creates a snapshot of volume's current proportional influence on price using volume-weighted moving averages (VWMA) versus simple moving averages (SMA).

CH 17 · KEY TAKEAWAYS

Core Insights

  • VPCI Contrasts VWMAs with SMAs
    Why it matters: While SMAs show price changes, VWMAs reflect investor participation. The difference exposes the intrinsic relationship between trend and volume.
  • VPCI Above Zero Confirms Uptrends
    Application: Positive numbers confirm an uptrend. Negative numbers confirm a downtrend. A rising VPCI while prices fall signals an imminent reversal (bullish contradiction). Ch 17, p. 185
  • Volume Leads Price Action
    Evidence: Unlike lagging price indicators, VPCI typically gives signals prior to price breaks. The March 2009 V-bottom in VPCI signaled the exact end of the bear market. Ch 17, p. 188
  • VPCI Consistently Outperforms OBV
    Evidence: Backtesting SPY (1993-2006) showed VPCI returning an 8.11% annualized return with a 0.74 Sharpe ratio, vastly outperforming OBV's -1.57% return. Ch 17, p. 202
CH 17 · MENTAL MODELS

Proportional Imbalance

Proportional Imbalance (Greed vs. Entropy)

A price trend requires proportional volume fuel to sustain itself; otherwise, it is driven by complacency or apathy rather than true conviction.
The Four States:
  • Invigorated Greed: Uptrend + Increasing Volume (Strong Demand). Market is supported by the fuel it needs to grow.
  • Complacency: Uptrend + Decreasing Volume (Weak Demand). Greed with entropy; the trend will break down as fuel dissipates.
  • Fear: Downtrend + Increasing Volume (Strong Supply). Fear is fed by panic selling energy.
  • Apathy: Downtrend + Decreasing Volume (Weak Supply). Fear with entropy; a slow, agonizing bleed.
⚠️ Limitations: Fear can be self-sustaining for long periods without volume (apathy), whereas greed requires a constant influx of new buyers (volume). Ch 17, p. 188
CH 17 · NOTABLE QUOTES

Fueling the Trend

"An uptrend with increasing volume is a market characterized by greed supported by the fuel it needs to grow. An uptrend without volume is complacent; greed deprived it of the fuel needed to sustain itself."

— Buff Dormeier, explaining the behavioral underpinnings of the VPCI. Ch 17, p. 186

Interpretation: Price movement alone doesn't guarantee the sustainability of a trend. Volume acts as the metabolic rate of the market. Without participation, an uptrend is hollow and vulnerable to the slightest selling pressure. Conversely, a fearful market can collapse under its own weight, but a greedy market requires fresh capital.

CH 17 · DEEP DIVE

The Mathematics of VPCI

The 3 Pillars of VPCI

  • VPC (Volume-Price Confirmation): VWMA(long) - SMA(long). The pure asymmetry between volume-weighted and simple price. Measures baseline alignment.
  • VPR (Volume-Price Ratio): VWMA(short) / SMA(short). Accentuates VPC relative to recent volume relationships. A value >1 increases the weight of the VPC.
  • VM (Volume Multiplier): AvgVol(short) / AvgVol(long). Overweights the indicator when volume is actively expanding.

Synthesis

VPCI = VPC × VPR × VM

Instead of merely smoothing out noise, VPCI acts as an amplifying filter. It magnifies the underlying price trend only when sustained participation (VWMA vs SMA) and short-term intensity (Volume Multiplier) validate the move.

Multi-Dimensional Analysis

  • Layer 2: Structural

  • Layer 3: Contextual

  • Layer 5: Generative
    VPCI doesn't just have to be a standalone oscillator. It can be used as a dynamically adjusting factor for stop losses (e.g., dividing your stop-loss distance by the VPCI to tighten stops automatically when complacency sets in). Ch 17, p. 204

Testing the Strategy

📊 Empirical Data

The Study: Tested a 5/20-day moving average crossover system from August 1996 to June 2004 across 60 stocks (spanning 12 subgroups of capitalization, volume, and volatility).

Results: Adding VPCI confirmation improved profitability in 65% of the issues (39 of 60), boosting overall profits by $211,997. The profit factor improved by 19% across the board.

Strengths: A wide array of market caps and styles were tested to prevent curve-fitting. Applied systematically across long and short sides without discretionary intervention.

Weaknesses: Excluded trading commissions and slippage. Tested on older data ending in 2004 (though fundamental volume principles are persistent).

CH 17 · COMPETING VIEWS

VPCI vs. OBV

On-Balance Volume (Granville)

Philosophy: Accumulates daily volume based on close direction (+ volume on up days, - volume on down days). Uncovers hidden "coils" in trendless markets.

Weakness: Accumulates historical baggage. A massive volume spike five years ago permanently skews the absolute OBV value today, making it slow to react to modern shifts.

Performance (SPY 1993-2006): Annualized Return -1.57%, Sharpe Ratio -0.09.

VPCI (Dormeier)

Philosophy: Provides a time-bound snapshot of volume's direct influence on price over specific moving averages. Evaluates trend health without holding permanent historical baggage.

Strength: Dynamic, adapts to recent market structure, filters out distant historical noise, and confirms existing trends rapidly.

Performance (SPY 1993-2006): Annualized Return +8.11%, Sharpe Ratio 0.74.

CH 17 · IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE

Systematic Implementation

1. Set Up Averages
2. Overlay VPCI
3. Identify Divergence
4. Risk Control

Systematic Implementation Workflow

  • Averages: Establish a long-term (e.g., 20-day or 50-day) and short-term (e.g., 5-day or 10-day) VWMA and SMA baseline in your charting software.
  • Overlay: Add VPCI, a smoothed average of VPCI, and a zero-line histogram.
  • Signals: Look for VPCI crossing above VPCI Smoothed while below zero as an early entry signal. Wait to confirm with price breaking a trendline to prevent false starts.
  • Risk Control: Divide your trailing stop distance by the raw VPCI. If VPCI diverges (goes negative while price is high), your stop automatically tightens, preserving capital before the drop.

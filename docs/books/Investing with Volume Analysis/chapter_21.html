CH 21 · MENTAL MODELS

The Market Positioning System

The Market Positioning System (MPS)

A framework to locate the exact position of the market or an individual stock based on two coordinates: Price Trend (x-axis) and Volume Breadth (y-axis).
When to Apply:
  • To gauge the broad market using Advance-Decline (x-axis) and Cap-Weighted Volume (y-axis).
  • To screen individual stocks using the Trend Thrust Indicator (TTI) (x-axis) and VPCI (y-axis).
⚠️ Limitations:
  • It evaluates current states, not future forecasts.
  • Stocks at the center (equilibrium) offer poor risk/reward compared to the quadrants.

CH 21 · DEEP DIVE

The 5-Step Process

The 5-Step Investment Process

Dormeier constructs a top-down funnel that filters market noise before individual stock selection.

  1. Direction of Trend: Identify the primary secular trend of the broad market. (Is the tide coming in or out?)
  2. Trend Sustainability: Verify the broad trend using the VPCI Index. Does volume confirm or contradict the price?
  3. Market Breadth: Construct a macro MPS. Compare the advance-decline line against cap-weighted up/down volume. Ideal state: upper-left quadrant (strong price breadth + strong volume breadth).
  4. Market Sectors: Employ relative strength analysis. Rank sectors by price-volume momentum (using VW-MACD) and their endurance in the top quartile.
  5. Individual Stock Selection: Drill down into specific issues using an individual stock MPS (TTI vs VPCI), focusing on extreme quadrants while avoiding the equilibrium center.
CH 21 · READING LAYERS

Reading Layers

Layer 1: Surface Reading

The author introduces a step-by-step strategy for analyzing the market top-down. The process relies on custom indicators like VPCI, TTI, and Cap-Weighted Volume to map market conditions.

Layer 2: Structural Reading

The logic is purely deductive. He establishes the macro rules (Step 1-3), narrows the scope to sectors (Step 4), and only then identifies the micro elements (Step 5). This prevents the common technical flaw of bottom-up bias.

Layer 3: Contextual Reading

The system borrows heavily from navigation metaphors (GPS, prime meridian, equator). This makes complex multi-variable analysis accessible, attempting to bridge the gap between institutional quant models and retail traders.

Layer 4: Critical Reading

While the logic is sound, the reliance on proprietary or highly specific indices (like the VPCI Index and TTI) makes it difficult for a layperson to implement without specialized software (like Worden's TeleChart). The empirical tests presented are over a very brief window (Jan-Apr 2010).

Layer 5: Generative Reading

By conceptualizing technical indicators as geographical coordinates, we can extend the MPS to include a third dimension (Z-axis), such as institutional order flow, options gamma exposure, or macroeconomic liquidity, creating a 3D topographic map of modern market mechanics.

Evidence Evaluation

📊 Empirical Data

Evidence: The author tested S&P 500 components from Jan-Apr 2010. The most bullish volume index returned 10.41%, while the S&P 500 fell 2.04%. Stocks in the equilibrium center returned only 1.01%. Ch 21, p. 271

Strengths: It clearly proves the theoretical framework using real market data and quantifies exact returns for each MPS quadrant.

Weaknesses: The sample size is extremely small (only a 4-month period in a specific post-crash market condition). It lacks multi-year, full-cycle backtesting.

2026 Update: Modern algorithmic trading heavily exploits these exact price/volume extremes, confirming the validity, but also meaning retail investors may suffer from high slippage attempting to manually trade these corners.

CH 21 · COMPETING VIEWS

Competing Views

Dormeier's MPS View

Core Claim: Top-down, multi-factor volume and price trend mapping is essential. Only trade when the macro, sector, and individual stock quadrants align.

Assumption: Volume leads price, and macro breadth dictates the sustainability of individual stock movements.

Pure Price Action (e.g., Al Brooks)

Core Claim: All necessary information is contained in the price bar itself. Broad market macro analysis or sector rotation is a distraction for active traders.

Assumption: The tape is the ultimate truth, and volume or breadth data often lags or complicates execution unnecessarily.

Synthesis: The two views serve different timeframes. Dormeier's MPS is optimized for swing traders and investors looking for multi-week/month trends, whereas pure price action serves intraday scalpers where macro breadth shifts are too slow to matter.
CH 21 · COUNTERARGUMENTS

Counterarguments

Potential Flaws in the Strategy

1. The "Small Sample Size" Critique

The statistical validity of the 4-month study in 2010 is questionable. Markets undergo distinct regimes (high volatility vs low volatility, QE vs QT). A framework validated in a brief sideways-to-down market might underperform in a massive liquidity-driven bull run.

2. Over-Complication

By requiring the alignment of five separate layers (Broad trend, VPCI Index, MPS Breadth, VW-MACD Sectors, TTI/VPCI Individual), the system risks analysis paralysis. An investor might miss entire market moves waiting for the perfect alignment of all five steps.

3. Data Latency

Calculating the aggregate VPCI and TTI for every stock in the S&P 500 is computationally intensive. In modern markets (2026), HFT algorithms price in these volume discrepancies in milliseconds, potentially arbitraging away the edge before a retail swing trader can calculate their MPS map.

Implementation Guide

Step-by-Step Implementation

  • Phase 1: Setup: Configure your charting software to track the Advance-Decline line, Cap-Weighted Volume, VW-MACD for sectors, and VPCI/TTI for individual stocks.
  • Phase 2: Broad Scan (Weekly): Evaluate the broad market. Plot the MPS coordinates. Are we in the upper-left quadrant (bullish)? If not, size down positions or move to cash.
  • Phase 3: Deep Scan (Daily): Identify the top 3 sectors via VW-MACD momentum. Screen stocks within those sectors to find those entering the Upper-Left (Overbought + Bullish Volume) or exiting the Lower-Right (Oversold V-Bottom).
  • Phase 4: Execute: Avoid the equilibrium middle. Only initiate trades when the stock hits the extreme corners of the MPS. Use the Anti-Volume Stop Loss (from Ch 20) to manage risk.
